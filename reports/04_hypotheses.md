# Research Backlog — better-motivated hypotheses (round 2)

After six naive rules were honestly killed (`reports/03_discovery_report.md`), the prime
directive calls for **fewer, better-motivated hypotheses, not more searching**. These were
generated by a mechanism-first ideation pass and each is (a) grounded in a real market
mechanism, (b) testable on the MNQ/MES 1-min data we already have, and (c) explicitly
**drift-controlled** — the failure mode of `orb_long` (a 0.87 Sharpe that was just
bull-market beta). All three diagnose the same flaw in plain ORB: **it fired every day
regardless of regime.**

## H1 — Compression-Gated ORB  ★ implementing first
- **Mechanism:** volatility is autocorrelated/mean-reverting at the daily horizon. A *quiet*
  overnight (small ETH range vs the 20-day ATR of the RTH range) means price has coiled in a
  tight balance with resting liquidity at the edges; when it finally breaks there is little
  opposing size and an inventory/stop-run cascade follows. This is a coiled spring, not drift.
- **Rule:** arm the day only if `overnight_range / ATR20 <= q`. If armed, take the first
  15-min opening-range breakout (buffer `b` ticks), hold to a stop (fraction of OR width) or
  the 15:00 close. Direction chosen intraday → **symmetric long/short**.
- **Why not drift:** a rising market cannot generate the short trades; arming (overnight
  compression) is orthogonal to direction. Honesty check: long-leg vs short-leg P&L should
  both contribute.
- **Likely failure:** armed days are rare (~25–35%); after 2-tick costs the sample may starve
  the 200-trade / DSR gates. _(scan: q∈{.2,.3,.4}, b∈{1,2,4}, stop∈{.5,1.0} → ~18 trials)_

## H2 — Failed-Breakout ORB **Fade** (balanced regime)
- **Mechanism:** on rotational days the opening-range poke is a *liquidity trap* — late
  breakout traders get trapped and cover, reverting price into the range. Fade the *failed*
  breakout, gated by a narrow prior-day range and weak opening-bar volume.
- **Why not drift:** net short up-pokes / long down-pokes, flat overnight → ~zero net beta; in
  a pure uptrend a naive fade would *lose*, so positive expectancy must be real reversion.
- **Likely failure:** brutal negative skew — the ~20% of true trend days can erase months of
  small wins; PBO risk from easy-to-overfit fade thresholds.

## H3 — Gap + value-location-gated ORB
- **Mechanism:** auction theory — a breakout's information depends on *where* the day opens vs
  prior value. Gap beyond prior-day RTH range AND opening range entirely outside prior value =
  genuine repricing (acceptance); continue in the gap direction. Open inside prior value = noise.
- **Why not drift:** armed on gap **sign** (down-gaps arm shorts), symmetric; gap is
  information-driven, not trend-driven.
- **Likely failure:** sample scarcity (large gaps that stay out-of-value are a few % of days);
  up-gaps dominate the 2019–2026 sample so the long leg could smuggle in drift — MCPT must decide.

### H4 — Overnight-Inventory Gap-Fade  ★ next to implement
- **Mechanism:** fade the RTH open's displacement from prior RTH close back toward prior value
  (gap-fill / inventory mean-reversion). Sign set by the gap (gap-up → short, gap-down → long),
  decided at the open → **sign-symmetric**, flat overnight.
- **Why not drift:** payoff is orthogonal to trend (target = prior close, not a directional ride);
  abundant sample (~40–55% of days arm) directly fixes the starvation that killed conditional rules.
- **Mandate:** roll-gap handling up front (skip arming on the first ~2 sessions after each quarterly
  roll, or compute the gap in adj space) — the contamination that sank the earlier gap ideas. Judge
  on the **per-year leg gate**, not pooled P&L. Expected ~450–600 trades.
- **Grid (≤16):** `or_bars∈{1,3}`, `buffer_ticks∈{2,4}`, `g_hi∈{0.5,0.8}` (upper gap-size band in
  prior-range units), `stop_mult∈{1.0,1.5}`; `g_lo`, `ext`, `target=PC` fixed.

### H5 — (lower priority) double-rejection failed-breakout reversal
- Only the **double-poke "poor high/low"** variant of H2 is defensible (the naive midpoint fade lost
  gross). Cap risk well inside one OR width; require a no-trend filter. **Sample risk is high** —
  kill on a smoke run if trades < 200 before spending a full-gate run.
