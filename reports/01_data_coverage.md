## Integrity checks (back-adjusted continuous MNQ)

- Index monotonic, **0 duplicate timestamps, 0 NaNs**.
- 1-min log-return std **0.00033**; max |move| **4.3%** (a real 2020/2022 vol bar); **0 bars > 5%**.
- **Roll continuity verified**: median |return| at the 29 roll boundaries is **0.00053**

  series. Those synthetic jumps are removed by additive (Panama) back-adjustment —
  the unadjusted series is retained for absolute-price logic.

## Will this work? — Yes, for the futures-first program

- **MNQ is the rigor center**: ~7.1 years of clean 1-min is ample for in-sample MCPT,
  multi-year walk-forward, purged CPCV, and PBO. This is the primary research instrument.
- **MES (~6 years)** gives a correlated second instrument for MES–MNQ spread work and
  portfolio diversification, and an out-of-instrument robustness check.

## What is NOT covered (honest gaps)

- **ES / NQ history is essentially absent** (only 1 week of ES). True ES–NQ intermarket /
  cointegration edges need full ES + NQ minute history. MES/MNQ is a micro proxy for the
  same index exposure, so we can still study the **MES–MNQ** relationship — but classic
  big-contract ES–NQ lead/lag is out of reach until that data is exported.
- **No tick data here.** Genuine microstructure (VPIN, Kyle's λ, true imbalance/run bars)
  needs trade-level data. On this minute data, microstructure features are **minute-bar
  proxies** and will be labelled as such. (Your NinjaTrader `db/tick/` had ~1yr of MNQ
  tick — exportable later for a microstructure sub-study.)
- **No FX here** (deferred Phase 5).
